Reaching Your Potential through Self-Advocacy

Welcome to Reaching Your Potential through Self-Advocacy! In this course, you will understand how self-advocacy increases your self-awareness and learn strategies to build this skill for yourself and with your students.

About This Course

Advocating for one's self is no simple task. It is a life skill that needs support and practice. From an early age, we are faced with challenges that only we can solve. Developing and nurturing self-advocacy not only helps you navigate life's challenges but can also change your life. Get ready to utilize a process that will help you know yourself a little better and give you strategies to build this skill for yourself and with your students.

Course Format

The content of this course is centered on a three-part process of building self-advocacy:

1. Understanding one's needs

2. Knowing what supports might help

3. Communicating those needs with others

While you learn more about each part of the process, you'll have the opportunity to explore strategies to implement and practice. You'll also be invited to reflect on a current challenge to consider how the process of self-advocacy can help you determine what you need to move forward.

Module: Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Self-Transcendence

The discovery of one's sense of meaning.

Self-Actualization

Use of talents, development of abilities, and achieving one's potential.

Esteem

Recognition, attention, and feeling of accomplishment.

Social Belonging

Family, companionship, and sense of belonging or connection to others.

Safety Needs

Personal security, emotional security, and financial security.

Physiological Needs

Shelter, food, water, sleep, clothes, exercise, etc.
